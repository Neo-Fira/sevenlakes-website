Sixth Lake of Seven Lakes: Marguzor

Marguzor is often seen as the most visually powerful lake in the system: wider, larger and more open than the earlier stops.

Lake overview

Marguzor stands out for its wide water surface, mountain reflections and broad open views. Many travelers spend their longest stop here and take their strongest panoramic photos.

Location and altitude

Location: Upper part of the cascade before Hazorchashma.

Altitude: around 2140 m.

The altitude is shown as approximate because small differences appear across guidebooks and travel sources.

Why this lake matters

Marguzor stands out for its wide water surface, mountain reflections and broad open views. Many travelers spend their longest stop here and take their strongest panoramic photos.

After Marguzor the route rises toward Hazorchashma, the highest lake in the cascade.

Travel tips

These tips help you understand this lake better and plan the full Haft Kul route with more confidence.

Practical advice

  • Allow more time here if you want to photograph calmly and explore the surroundings.
  • The best frames usually include both the lake and the lines of the surrounding slopes.
  • Marguzor pairs naturally with the photo guide and the full Seven Lakes route.
