Second Lake of Seven Lakes: Soya

Soya feels cooler and deeper in color because the surrounding rock walls hold more shade and make the water look denser.

Lake overview

The second lake often looks darker than the first because of terrain and shadow. That contrast helps visitors understand why all seven lakes feel like separate landscape scenes rather than repeated viewpoints.

Location and altitude

Location: Shing Valley, between the first and third lakes.

Altitude: around 1701 m.

The altitude is shown as approximate because small differences appear across guidebooks and travel sources.

Why this lake matters

The second lake often looks darker than the first because of terrain and shadow. That contrast helps visitors understand why all seven lakes feel like separate landscape scenes rather than repeated viewpoints.

After Soya the road continues to Hushyor. Together the first lakes show how quickly the water color, shade and shape of the valley can change.

Travel tips

These tips help you understand this lake better and plan the full Haft Kul route with more confidence.

Practical advice

  • Compare the water color with Mijgon to see the contrast clearly.
  • A few steps above the shore often give the better angle.
  • When it is windy, shorter photo stops work better than one long stop.
